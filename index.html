Hello, I am a second year M.S. student in KAIST Graduate school of AI.
I am advised by Professor Se-Young Yun and a member of OSI Lab. I'm also collaborating closely with Professor Kimin Lee.

My research focuses on pushing the boundaries of generative models in terms of applicability, while maintaining reliability. Currently, I am focusing on scalable and robust post-training of generative models, including both Large Language Models and Diffusion Models. My current projects involve: (1) enabling sequential decision-making for LLM agents through multi-agent systems and reinforcement learning, and (2) preventing reward over-optimization during alignment to enhance reliability. Recently, I have been particularly interested in LLM agents for data generation, aiming to enable the next step toward self-evolving systems.

Previously, I worked on domain- and task-adaptation using efficient fine-tuning as well as the reliability and safety of LLMs.
